Global Book Ratings and Reading Culture

User Age Distribution

Most readers fall between the ages of 20 and 35, making young adults the most active participants in online book reviewing platforms. Engagement declines steadily with age, particularly after 50, suggesting that older groups participate less in digital reading communities. The strong concentration of users in their 20s and early 30s reflects a tech-savvy, digitally engaged demographic driving modern reading trends. Lower participation among seniors may reflect less digital adoption, rather than less interest in reading itself.

Global Book Distribution Map

Regions like South America and Africa display higher average ratings, indicating more active reviewing communities and engaged reading cultures. This reflects consistent participation and a strong sense of literary enthusiasm in these areas. In contrast, lighter regions such as Australia and parts of Asia may indicate fewer reviews or more critical readers. By comparing scores geographically, the map reveals cultural differences in rating behaviour and highlights where global reading communities are most vibrant. Some regions rate books more leniently, while others are more critical, showcasing distinct reading attitudes around the world.

Genre Evolution Over Time

The darker shades in recent decades reveal an expansion in global literary output, driven by easier publishing, digitalisation, and globalisation. The rise of genres like Business, Science, and Computers shows growing interest in modern, knowledge-driven topics, while traditional genres such as History and Religion remain stable but less dominant. Some genres, like Fiction, maintain consistent popularity, reflecting long-term reader loyalty. Overall, this visualisation captures how literary trends evolve alongside technological and cultural shifts.
